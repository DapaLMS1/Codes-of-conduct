Codes of Conduct and Practice Standards

While the Dental Board of Australia (DBA) Code of Conduct primarily targets registered practitioners, its principles apply to the conduct of all staff in a dental practice. Review the two sections below.

Section A: Key Areas of Conduct

Confidentiality and Privacy

Mandatory adherence to the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and state/territory health records legislation is required. Patient information (including clinical data, financial details, and personal conversations) must be protected. The principle is "need to know."

Informed Consent

The assistant's role is critical in ensuring the process of consent is followed, including documenting discussions, answering non-clinical questions within their scope, and ensuring the patient has the opportunity to ask questions.

Professional Boundaries

Maintaining appropriate professional relationships with patients. This includes avoiding dual relationships and refraining from any personal or financial exploitation of the professional relationship.

Working within Scope of Practice

Dental assistants must strictly adhere to the tasks and procedures they are trained, competent, and legally authorised to perform. Performing tasks outside of this scope (unauthorised practice) is a serious ethical and legal violation.

Section B: Professional Responsibilities

Infection Control

Strict compliance with the current Australian Guidelines for the Prevention and Control of Infection in Healthcare is non-negotiable and a critical component of non-maleficence and duty of care.

Accurate Documentation

All actions, patient observations, and communications must be recorded contemporaneously, accurately, and legibly.

Cultural Safety

Practising in a way that respects and is sensitive to the cultural identity of patients, addressing power imbalances, and advocating against discrimination.
